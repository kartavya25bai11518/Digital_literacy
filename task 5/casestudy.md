Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

Introduction:
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly increasing form of cybercrime in India, where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions through mobile payment apps such as Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm.

How the Crime Happens (Step-by-Step):

The fraudster contacts the victim via call, SMS, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, seller, or customer.
They create a sense of urgency (e.g., refund, prize, or account issue).
The victim is asked to “receive money” by approving a collect request or scanning a QR code.
In reality, the request is for sending money, not receiving it.
Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money is instantly transferred to the fraudster’s account.

Targeted Victims:
Common targets include students, elderly people, small business owners, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payment systems.

Consequences:
Victims may suffer immediate financial loss, often with little chance of recovery due to the instant nature of UPI transactions. Additionally, such incidents can cause emotional stress, loss of trust in digital platforms, and reluctance to use online payment systems.

Conclusion:
UPI fraud highlights the importance of digital awareness. Users should never share their UPI PIN or approve unknown requests, ensuring safer online transactions.
