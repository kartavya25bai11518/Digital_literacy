Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)

#General Online Safety
1.Use strong, unique passwords
Create passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password across apps like email, banking, and social media.
2.Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Turn on 2FA for important accounts (Gmail, Instagram, banking apps) to add an extra layer of security.
3.Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities
Don’t log into banking or payment apps on public Wi-Fi networks (cafés, hostels) without a VPN.
4.Be cautious of phishing messages
Do not click on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp claiming prizes, job offers, or urgent account issues.
5.Download apps only from official sources
Use Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APK files from unknown websites.
6.Limit personal information sharing
Don’t overshare details like phone number, address, college ID, or travel plans on social media.

#Financial / UPI Safety (Important)
7.Never share UPI PIN or OTP
Banks and payment apps (like PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm) will NEVER ask for your PIN or OTP. Sharing it can lead to instant money loss.
8.Verify “collect requests” carefully
When receiving a UPI request, double-check the sender’s name. Remember:
  You enter your PIN to send money, not to receive it.
9.Avoid screen-sharing during transactions
Fraudsters may ask you to share your screen on apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer—this can expose your banking details.

#Device & App Safety
10.Keep your device updated
Regularly update your phone and apps to fix security vulnerabilities.
11.Install antivirus/security apps
Use trusted mobile security apps to detect malware and threats.
12.Log out from shared devices
Always log out of accounts when using college labs or shared systems.

#If You Face Cyber Fraud
+Immediately report on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
+Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (available across India)
+Inform your bank/payment app instantly to block transactions
